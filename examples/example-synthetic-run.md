# Example: what a synthetic run looks like

An abridged Route C output, run against the fictional demo persona
(`personas/example-mara-freelance-designer.md`) with a guide asking about chasing overdue
invoices. Note the two parts: the transcript, then the fidelity note. The fidelity note is not
optional decoration; it is the part that keeps the method honest.

---

## Part 1: Transcript

**Persona 01: Mara, freelance brand designer**

**Q: Tell me about the last invoice that went unpaid longer than you expected. Start wherever
feels natural.**

So, the agency. They're my biggest client, they subcontract overflow to me, and last spring an
$8,000 invoice sat for, and I will never forget this number, eighty-seven days. And the worst
part isn't even the money, it's that I sent two follow-ups in that time and both of them had a
smiley in them. Like, "just bumping this :)". Who am I?

**Q: What were you thinking when you wrote that second email?**

Honestly? "Don't be the difficult one." They feed me maybe a third of my year. If I send
something with an edge and the PM screenshots it to the partners, that's it. So you do the
smiley. The smiley is load-bearing.

**Q: You said the money isn't the worst part. Oh?**

I mean, it matters, rent matters. But the silence is the thing. You send the invoice and it's
just... nothing. I check my banking app like it's a slot machine. The work part of freelancing
I can do. It's the part where I become a collections agency with no leverage that nobody warned
me about.

**Q: Has there been a time when it went past silence?**

[pause] There was a startup guy. Full identity system, logo, deck, everything, delivered. Two
emails, I tried calling once. Nothing. Forty-two hundred dollars. And the thing people don't
get is, there's no next move that feels normal. Email three is what, a threat? A lawyer? Over
four grand? So it just... became a thing I don't talk about.

**Q: When that was happening, what did you wish you'd had?**

A version of me with no feelings. Like, something that knows the exact email to send on day 15
and day 30 and day 45, that sounds like me but doesn't lose sleep. But, and this is important,
it shows me before anything goes out. If something pushy goes to the agency without me seeing
it, I'm done. That's not a feature, that's a fireable offense.

## Part 2: Fidelity & caveats note

**Persona 01: Mara**

- **Well-grounded responses:** none in the real sense; this is a fictional demo persona. In a
  real run this section lists which answers trace to documented episodes in the persona file
  (e.g. "the 87-day story and the smiley detail come straight from §2, told to me by the real
  person it happened to").
- **Speculative responses:** the "day 15 / day 30 / day 45" cadence; the persona file says
  nothing about what schedule she'd accept. The "slot machine" banking-app line extends §4
  beyond what's written.
- **Validate-with-a-real-person list:**
  1. Is "draft yes, send no" the actual trust line, or would a real freelancer delegate sending
     for low-stakes clients?
  2. Does the fear of being "the difficult one" survive when the amount gets bigger? Where's
     the threshold?
  3. Is the dead-end after email two ("no normal next move") real, or do real freelancers
     escalate more than this persona assumes?
- **Persona gaps exposed:** §6 has no job covering "what happens after chasing fails"
  (write-off vs. escalation), and the interview kept bumping into it.

**Next:** refine the persona (Route B), run a comparative round with a second persona, or, once
at least one real interview exists, synthesize (Route D).
